Rest & Recovery

Rest days are as important as workout days. This is when your muscles repair, grow stronger, and recover. Don't skip them!

  • Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.
  • Eat nutrient-dense foods to fuel recovery.
  • Engage in light activity like walking or stretching.

Wrist Curls with a Dumbbell

Description

The wrist curl is a strength training exercise that targets the forearm flexors on the underside of your forearm. It is typically performed with a barbell or dumbbell while resting the forearms on the thighs or a bench.

Tip: Always Warm-up

Never skip your warm-up. A good 5-10 minutes of light cardio (like jumping jacks or jogging in place) followed by dynamic stretches (like arm circles) prepares your muscles for work, increases blood flow, and reduces the risk of injury.

Tip: Focus on Form

Proper form is more important than heavy weight. Bad form not only leads to injury but also means you aren't working the target muscle effectively. Start light, master the movement, and only then increase the weight. Focus on the mind-muscle connection.

Tip: Don't Forget to Cool-down

After your workout, take 5 minutes to cool down. This can include light stretching of the muscles you just worked. A cool-down helps bring your heart rate back to normal gradually and can aid in recovery by promoting blood flow and reducing muscle soreness.
